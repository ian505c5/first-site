Project Overview

For the final project of User Experience, groups were asked to concept and design a website that requires users to carry out some task. We chose to take the traditional model of online retail, but apply some innovative twists to make shopping online a more engaging experience.

Viktori Clothing

The Challenge

Traditionl online clothing retailers offer an experience that is one-dimensional. Consumers select items for purchase, and once payment is received, the retailer agrees to ship them. Pretty straightforward, but not so exciting. How then, do you add thrill to online shopping?

Our Thinking

Consumers engage with products that relate to them on some level. It could be the design of the shirt, the brand ideology, or that the item embodies some expression of their personality. Knowing this, what if the user had a say in what items the retailer made avaialble for sale? We think that if we empower the users to drive the style of our brand, in a fun and engaging context, consumers will ultimately want to own products of their own creation.

The Solution

Viktori Clothing is part retailer, part design community, and part Las Vegas. Each week, a competition is held between user submitted t-shirt designs. The designs with the most votes at the end of the week are sent to the printer. The remaining designs are discarded to either be resubmitted for the following competition or retouched.

The voting plays out a little differently that one might be used to. Everytime you vote on a t-shirt, you pledge $15 on the design. If the design wins, you get the shirt for the $15 you already pledged (a hold is placed on your payment). If you lose, the $15 hold is lifted, and the money is returned to your account. This is the Vegas element, not every shirt you vote on will win, but when they do, its exciting and not only do you get a shirt on discount, but you helped make it happen.
